Technology

About Technology:

Computer education in schools equips students with essential digital literacy and technological skills. The curriculum covers basic computer operations, internet safety, coding, and the use of software applications. By integrating technology into learning, students enhance their problem-solving and critical thinking abilities. Our program prepares students to navigate the digital world confidently and responsibly, fostering future-ready skills.

Curriculum Details:

The computer education curriculum for lower school students covers basic computer operations, internet safety, and introductory coding. It also includes lessons on using essential software applications like word processors and presentation tools. The curriculum aims to build digital literacy and problem-solving skills, preparing students for the increasingly digital world.

Activities:

Technology activities for students include introductory coding projects like creating simple animations or games. They also engage in building basic circuits and exploring robotics to understand hardware and software interactions. Using educational software, students learn to create documents, presentations, and spreadsheets. These activities enhance digital literacy, problem-solving skills, and creative thinking, preparing students for the digital age.
