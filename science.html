Science

About Science:

Science is the study of the natural world through observation and experimentation, fostering curiosity and critical thinking. It encompasses various disciplines such as biology, chemistry, and physics, helping students understand the principles governing life, matter, and energy. Through hands-on experiments and real-world applications, science education encourages analytical skills and problem-solving. It aims to inspire a lifelong interest in discovering and understanding the world around us.

Curriculum Details:

The science curriculum for lower school students covers fundamental concepts in biology, chemistry, and physics, fostering curiosity and analytical thinking. Students engage in hands-on experiments and activities to explore topics like plant and animal life, basic chemical reactions, and the principles of forces and motion. The curriculum emphasizes the scientific method, encouraging observation, hypothesis formation, and experimentation. Through real-world applications, students develop a foundational understanding of the natural world and scientific inquiry.

Activities:

Science activities for students include hands-on experiments such as growing plants to understand biology, and simple chemical reactions to explore chemistry. Physics concepts are taught through fun projects like building basic circuits and studying motion with DIY models. These activities foster curiosity, critical thinking, and a practical understanding of scientific principles.
